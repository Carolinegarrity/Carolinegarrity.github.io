Caroline Garrity

My passion for engineering started in the ninth grade after taking my first tech-based course offered at my high school. I have since been determined to take the knowledge obtained in my Principles of Engineering class, where I was the only female student, and apply them to real world issues and projects. Some of my independent studies have been focused on biomechanical engineering, specifically, prosthetics. My interest in this sub field has led me to visit Hugh Herr’s biomechatronics lab at the MIT Media Lab, and talk with several MIT PhD students, designing advanced prosthetics as well as basic cost effective prosthetics for third world countries. Eager to pursue my technical interests, this past summer I built a 3D printer and am designing and printing my own scale model prosthetics. Besides my engineering and computer software interests, I love being a part of my varsity field hockey team and traveling to remote places such as Iceland, pictured here.
